CARS-ANTI-CATS Project

A community-driven solution to protect stray cats and ensure safety

About the Project

CAC（CARS-ANTI-CATS) is a community project that combined system design with animal protection means. The purpose of CAC system is to prevent stray cats from accidentally entering parts of a vehicle’s structure while seeking for warmthh, which could result in injury or death. Additionally, it aims to avoid potential operational malfunctions caused by such incidents within the vehicle.

How It Works

The system is based on Raspberry Pi 4, which detects the presence of a cat near a vehicle using infrared (IR) sensors and triggers an ultrasonic sound emitter to deter the cat. The system should increase the sound intensity if multiple sensors detect the cat simultaneously.


Component:

Component:

Raspberry Pi 4: The central control unit processes inputs from the infrared sensors and controls the ultrasonic emitter.

Infrared Sensors (7 units): Positioned around the vehicle to detect motion. Each sensor outputs a HIGH signal when a cat is detected and LOW when not.

Ultrasonic Sound Emitter: Emits ultrasonic sound when activated. The intensity of the sound increases based on the number of sensors detecting the cat.

Battery supply: The system draws power from the vehicle’s 12V battery through a step-down module (DC-DC converter) to convert the voltage to 5V backup supply lithium battery is required if the vehicle battery is not supplied.

Community Engagement

Beyond the technical solution, this project involved active collaboration with the local community. Through presentations in residential groups and discussions with frequent feeders of stray cats, I garnered support and ideas. With permission from property management, we placed insulated shelter in 7 seperate spot. At the same time, coorperation with frequent cat feeders alow us to conduct TNR(trap-neuter-release), allowing control of the stray cat population.

Impact

The impact of this project has been significant, both for the community and the stray cats. By installing insulated shelters, we've provided these cats with a safe and warm place to stay during the cold winter months, reducing the risks of injury or harm from cars. The TNR program has been successful in managing the stray population, preventing uncontrolled growth, and improving the overall health and well-being of the cats.


Additionally, many of the cats involved in the program have found loving homes through adoption, giving them a chance for a better life. On a larger scale, this project has raised awareness about the issue of stray animals in urban areas and has encouraged more community involvement in animal welfare. The collaboration with local residents, property management, and animal advocates has fostered a sense of shared responsibility and compassion, making the community a safer place for both people and animals.
