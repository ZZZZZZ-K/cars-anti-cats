CARS-ANTI-CATS Project

A community-driven solution to protect stray cats and ensure safety

About the Project

The CARS-ANTI-CATS (CAC) system is an innovative solution designed to prevent stray cats from seeking warmth under car engines, reducing accidents and ensuring the safety of both cats and vehicles. The system uses infrared sensors to detect cats and emits harmless sound waves to deter them. This project combines technology and empathy to address a significant community concern.

About How it works Community Impact

How It Works

The system is powered by car batteries and features infrared sensors for precise detection. Ultrasonic emitters were calibrated after extensive testing to ensure the safety and comfort of the cats, balancing effectiveness and welfare.

Community Engagement

Beyond the technical solution, this project involved active collaboration with the local community. Through presentations in residential groups and discussions with frequent feeders of stray cats, I garnered support and ideas. With permission from property management, insulated shelters were placed in approved locations.

Impact and Vision

The CARS-ANTI-CATS project highlights the power of combining technology and compassion to solve real-world problems. It reflects the importance of shared concerns driving collective action and showcases how innovation can be used to create sustainable solutions.
