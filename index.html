A community-driven solution to protect stray cats and ensure safety

About the Project

CAC（CARS-ANTI-CATS) is a community project that combined system design with animal protection means. The purpose of CAC system is to prevent stray cats from accidentally entering parts of a vehicle’s structure, particularly for warmth, which could result in injury or death. Additionally, it aims to avoid potential operational malfunctions caused by such incidents within the vehicle.

In the winter of 2022, I noticed a concerning issue in my community: stray cats were frequently seeking warmth under cars and inside engine compartments. Many residents reported seeing cats running around the underground parking garage, and some even thought they had accidentally hit a cat. As a member of both the automotive club and the animal protection society at school, I was motivated to find a solution to this problem. How It Works

The system is powered by car batteries and features infrared sensors for precise detection. Ultrasonic emitters were calibrated after extensive testing to ensure the safety and comfort of the cats, balancing effectiveness and welfare.

Community Engagement

By promoting this project within the local community, we have seen a strong positive response, with over 300 people now participating in the TNR initiative, as well as community workshops focused on understanding the importance of keeping strays safe.

Impact

The CAC project has made a significant impact on both local wildlife protection efforts and the community. Not only has it helped reduce the number of accidents caused by stray cats hiding in car engines, but it has also raised awareness about the need to care for stray animals and prevent harm. In addition to offering immediate solutions like insulated cat shelters, we’ve also introduced programs to educate the community about TNR (Trap, Neuter, Return) practices, which have successfully reduced the local stray cat population.

Furthermore, the project’s collaborative nature has helped build stronger bonds between local residents, animal welfare organizations, and car enthusiasts. By engaging with the community, we’ve seen a wave of support, both financially and in terms of volunteer efforts.

Ultimately, CAC aims to spread across more regions, helping to ensure that stray cats are safe and that their presence does not cause any harm to vehicles or to themselves.
